How to create a profile that will get you hired.


LinkedIn is a professional social networking platform. It allows you to look for jobs, for companies to find new employees and for you to stay connected and up-to-date about your industry. When searching for a job, having a strong LinkedIn profile is just as important as a resume.

Use a professional photo

  • Good Shots

  • Bad Shots

  • Use the name that is on your resume
  • Update your location
  • Use English while you are in an English-speaking context (plus your native language, if you like)

    • LinkedIn automatically inserts your previous or current job title or education status, but you should change that. Use a title that shows what you want to be known for.

    Use searchable keywords

    Here are a few examples to give you some inspiration:

    Hospitality professional with Front of House Operations experience
    Customer Relations Specialist | B2B Sales | Team Leader
    Food and Beverage Service professional | Banquet Service Trained
    Guest Services expert with experience in large scale hotels/boutique residences/corporate sales and marketing
    Financial Management Enthusiast | Not-for-profit organization volunteer

    Your LinkedIn summary depends on your objective. What is your passion and how does it drive your goal?

  • Tell a brief professional life story (give examples). It is much more personal than your resume objective.

  • Specify whether you are open to relocation or remote work

  • Use bullet points and short sentences

  • Speak in the first person



    • Here is an example:
    I have always been passionate about connecting with others and expanding my world view. I believe that we learn and grow
    from our interactions with others. For this reason, my career path has been in the hospitality industry, where I can
    undertake many different roles and each day is a little different.
    • I was promoted to Customer Service Team Leader within 6 months because of my customer satisfaction results

    • I am praised for my motivation, professional attitude and ability to think fast!

    • I thrive in a role that allows me to see real results and achieve positive feedback

    • Experienced in CRM software, Oracle and onboarding new team members


    Keen to join a team that works together to provide memorable and outstanding service.

    ________________________________
    Planning, organization and supporting others have always been strengths that I am proud of.
    I am an administrator with experience in various industries, various management levels and demands. It has given me
    excellent insight into what is most effective to ensure smooth processes and great communication for all. I am confident
    that this dynamic and broad experience has given me a formula to carry with me into any job role.
    I always want to improve and learn new ways of doing things, to continue learning new skills.
    Experience and Education
    Your Education and Experience sections should reflect what is on your resume.

  • List your current and previous jobs, in reverse chronological order

  • Find the exact company with the correct logo if you can

  • List all of your relevant experiences that relate to your goal

  • 10 years’ maximum

  • List some responsibilities, not everything


    • Skills

    Don’t skip this section! You can add up to 50 skills. It will help recruiters find you based on the skills on your profile
    - you will appear in more searches each week!
    LinkedIn will also recommend jobs to you, based on the skills on your profile.

    If you are not sure what to write:
  • Look at the skills on someone who has a job you would like to do

  • Look at the skills and requirements on a job description that interests you


    • You can also ask friends, coworkers and classmates to endorse your skills (confirm that they are true!)

    Recommendations
    Have you ever written a review on Google for a product or a service? Well imagine the same thing, but you are the product!
    Ask a coworker, classmate, instructor or ex-colleague to write a few good words about you and your abilities.

    Top tips!
  • Be proactive… Consistently!

  • Update your settings and privacy

  • Send a message when your connect with someone or after they accept.

  • “Hi, I noticed that you have really interesting experience in the xxxxxx industry. I would love to connect as we have a similar background and field of work. Have a great week.”

  • Write posts, share posts, share articles

  • If you have an online portfolio….. share it!

  • Include your LinkedIn profile in your resume, cover letter and emails to employers
