Resume Writing


A resume is a professional document. It is a summary of your professional background, education and skills.
A resume is essential if you want to apply for a job. A strong resume gives you a competitive advantage!
If you have never written a resume before… keep reading.

Your resume should:
Be 1 - 2 pages, maximum. Do not send a resume that is more than 2 pages.
Be easy to read. Do not overload the page with too much writing. Do not leave huge blank spaces. Organize the information on the page in a way that the brain can understand it easily*
*it has been found that human eyes naturally read information in an ‘E’ shape. Use a professional format. Use Arial, Verdana or Calibri font. Avoid lots of colors and pictures. Include information relevant to the job you apply to. Yes, that means you should customize your resume for every job application. Highlight the skills and experiences that are relevant to the job description only.
Have correct spelling and grammar. Ask someone to check your spelling and grammar before you send your resume. We recommend using Grammarly.com for excellent grammar support!

>Personal Summary / Objective / Professional Highlights

Imagine you have 15 seconds to explain your professional background, strengths and goal. That’s what
you should write here! Approximately 50 words.
A resume objective is 1 - 2 sentences to explain your goal and something you can offer the employer.
You can explain your target industry, role or company.

Professional highlights can be a list of 3 - 5 achievements and skills that you are well-known for,
that are directly relevant for the job you want.

>Work Experience / Employment History / Professional Background

Always write your most recent or current employment first. Only include 10 years of work history.

    Nearly all companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to filter through resumes. These systems scan through resumes to
    look for specific keywords, such as certain skills necessary for the job.
    The ATS summarizes what your skills are based on the keywords you chose. If the skills section of your resume doesn’t exactly
    match the job description, you will not be chosen for an interview.
    75% of resumes are rejected before a human reads them, simply because they do not contain the important information.
    Make it easy for recruiters to read and understand your skills!
    The most common skills that employers are looking for:

    • Knowledge of specific computer software and applications [MS Office, Quickbooks, Adobe Photoshop, CRM, Oracle, etc]
    • Collaboration / teamwork
    • Adaptability
    • Leadership
    • Communication
    • Language abilities
    • Customer service orientation
    • Planning and organization
    • Time management
    • Emotional Intelligence
    • Critical thinking and problem-solving
    • Initiative and good judgement

    ALWAYS check the job description for the professional skills the employer is looking for. Do they match your own?
    Don’t know what your skills are, or why? Ask someone! Ask a coworker, friend, classmate or instructor for feedback.
    People will be happy to share the great things about you.
    There are many, we promise. Here, it is important to STAND OUT as a unique candidate. Be someone that people WANT and NEED on their team!



    TIP!
    For every skill you write on your resume, you must have at least 1 example of when and how you used this skill.
    Reflect the language in the job description in your own resume / application

    You do not need to include hobbies or interests in your resume.
    However, if you think that your interests are related to the job, then it could be a good idea to include them.
    If you are applying for a company that sells outdoor clothing and equipment, and you enjoy climbing and running marathons… write that!
    If you are applying for a travel agency position and you have travelled to 20 countries… write that!
    If you are applying for a kitchen position and you won an award for your bakery skills… write that!
